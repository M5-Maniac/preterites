El Imperfecto — Rules

Used for ongoing/habitual past actions, descriptions, and background context. All verbs are regular EXCEPT ir, ser, and ver — and there are NO stem changes!

📌 When to use the Imperfect

Was + -ing form  (e.g. she was eating)  |  Used to + verb  (e.g. I used to play)
• Habitual/repeated past actions (trigger words like siempre, todos los días)
• Descriptions of how things were (appearance, feelings, mental states)
• Background setting / ongoing condition in the past
• Age, time, weather in the past

⭐ Key Rules

• 1S and 3S forms are identical — subject pronoun is often included to clarify who's doing the action
• NO stem changes in the imperfect — even verbs irregular in present/preterit are regular here
• ALL -ar, -er, -ir verbs are regular in the imperfect — EXCEPT ir, ser, and ver
• Verbs can be translated as "was/were + -ing" OR "used to + verb"

⚠️ -CAR / -GAR / -ZAR — Spelling Change in YO form ONLY

-CAR → QUÉ  buscar → yo busqué  |  -GAR → GUÉ  pagar → yo pagué  |  -ZAR → CÉ  organizar → yo organicé

Other examples: llegar → llegué · practicar → practiqué · marcar → marqué · tocar → toqué · empezar → empecé · comenzar → comencé · explicar → expliqué · sacar → saqué

Stem Changers in the Preterite

📌 Stem Changer Rules

• -AR stem changers NEVER stem change in preterite (use regular -AR endings)
• -ER stem changers NEVER stem change in preterite (use regular -ER endings)
• -IR stem changers DO stem change — but ONLY in 3S and 3P:
   – e → i  (e.g. servir: sirvió, sirvieron)
   – o → u  (e.g. dormir: durmió, durmieron)
   – Use regular -IR endings everywhere else
